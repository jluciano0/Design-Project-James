The Irishman (2019)
This slow paced flick by Martin Scorsese shows the life of Frank Sheeran and how he gains his ties with mob boss Russell Bufalino and later the infamous labor leader Jimmy Hoffa.
Schindler's List (1993)
Steven Spielberg's gut-wrenching film shows how terrible and inhumane The Holocaust was and the brutality demonstrated by the perpetrators. It also shows us a man who disobeyed his country's orders to do what he believed was right.
Scarface (1983)
Brian De Palma's dramatic take on the drug trade in Miami starring Al Pacino is a gruesome yet entertaining film with a fantastic set of characters, most infamously Tony Montana, a Cuban refugee determined to achieve the American Dream!
The Deer Hunter (1978)
Michael Cimino's brilliant portrayal of the Vietnam War depicts the consequences and long-term effects PTSD can have on its victims and what war really does to a person.
